Title

Efficient Management and Optimization of Data in Pub-Sub Networks suitable for Edge-supported IoT Systems

Abstract

Modern networking approaches including Internet of Things (IoT), Edge Computing, Publisher-Subscriber (Pub-Sub) networks enable handling of data effectively and locally at any scale and allow the efficient management and optimization. In Pub-Sub networks, brokers are intermediaries facilitating data delivery based on subscribed topics. However, the geographic distribution of brokers in multi-broker environments often leads to service disparities and intolerable delivery delays during peak events. This talk will highlight a few solutions to address the challenges due to broker-centric data delivery. One of the models proposes a topic-aware low latency load balancing approach that leverages Trie data structures for topic association, outlier detection for identifying hot topics, and sojourn time modeling for load balancing. However, the optimization of Pub-Sub networks underscores the importance of efficient data management strategies across diverse networking environments. To address challenges in networking paradigms while advancing the frontier of efficient data orchestration by seamlessly integrating load balancing strategies in Pub-Sub networks with innovative caching solutions in edge-assisted IoT frameworks.
